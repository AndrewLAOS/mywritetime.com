Privacy Policy

Collexicon  ·  Last Updated: May 17, 2026

This policy explains what information Collexicon collects, why, and how it's used. We've tried to keep it readable — no walls of legalese. If you have questions, feel free to reach out at any time.

1. Information We Collect

Collexicon is a vocabulary and word discovery app. We only collect information that's genuinely needed to make the app work well for you.

App data you create: Things like your saved word collection, streak history, quiz progress, and any preferences or settings you adjust. This data is stored locally on your device and stays there — it's yours.

Technical data collected automatically: Like most apps, Collexicon collects a small amount of technical information automatically. This includes things like device type, operating system version, general app usage patterns, and crash or error reports. This data helps us keep the app stable, fix bugs, and improve performance. It is not used to build a profile about you or target you with ads.

What we don't collect: We do not collect your location, contacts, photos, browsing history, or any sensitive personal information. We do not track you across other apps or websites.

2. How We Use Your Information

Here's what we actually use your data for:

  • Keeping your app working: Your saved words, streaks, and quiz history are stored locally so the app remembers your progress between sessions.
  • Fixing things that break: Crash reports and error logs help us find and squash bugs quickly.
  • Understanding how the app is used: Aggregate, anonymized usage patterns help us understand which features are working well and which might need attention.
  • Security: Detecting and preventing abuse or unauthorized access.

We don't use your data for advertising. We don't sell it. We don't share it with data brokers. Full stop.

3. Data Storage & Security

Your word collection, streaks, and quiz progress are stored locally on your device. We don't upload your personal app data to external servers. Any technical data that is transmitted (such as anonymized crash reports) is sent over encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS).

Because your app data lives on your device, you're in control of it. If you delete the app, that data is removed along with it.

4. Third-Party Services

To make Collexicon work reliably, we use a small number of third-party services. These are reputable providers, and they only receive information necessary for their specific function.

  • Analytics & crash reporting: We may use a lightweight analytics or crash-reporting tool to understand how the app performs. Any data sent to these services is anonymized or aggregated where possible.

We don't use third-party advertising networks or sell data to any third party for marketing purposes.

5. Children's Privacy

Collexicon is a vocabulary learning app suitable for all ages, including younger learners. We take children's privacy seriously.

We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under the age of 13 (or the applicable age of digital consent in your region) beyond what is strictly necessary for the app to function. We do not use children's data for analytics or behavioral profiling, and we do not display third-party advertising.

If you're a parent or guardian and you believe your child has provided personal information through the app without your knowledge, please contact us and we'll remove it promptly.

Parents and guardians are encouraged to supervise app usage and reach out with any questions or concerns about how we handle data for younger users.

6. Your Choices & Rights

You're in control of your information. Here's what you can do:

  • Delete your local data: You can clear your word collection, streak history, and other app data at any time by deleting the app from your device.
  • Opt out of analytics: Depending on your device settings, you may be able to limit app tracking or analytics. On iOS, you can adjust these in Settings → Privacy & Security.

If you're located in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, California, or another jurisdiction with specific privacy rights, you may have additional rights under applicable law (such as the right to object to processing or to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority). We honor these rights regardless of where you're located.

7. Changes to This Policy

If we ever make meaningful changes to this policy — especially anything that affects how your data is used — we'll let you know through the app before the changes take effect. Minor updates (like clarifying wording or updating contact info) may be made without a formal notice.

The date at the top of this page always reflects when it was last revised.
